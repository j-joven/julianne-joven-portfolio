About

My name is Julianne Joven and I am a graphic designer based in Virginia.

Currently, I am pursuing a BFA in graphic design at George Mason University. Throughout my time studying at George Mason, I have been able to acquire and cultivate skills within Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, and Photoshop. Additionally, I have created promotional graphics for the Filipino Cultural Association (FCA) and KDC Mythic, increasing visibility for both college organizations as well as support and turnout for their events and projects. As I continue to grow as a designer, I aim to develop a more versatile skill set and collaborate with more clients and experienced designers to further establish my brand and expertise.

I did not start out as a digital artist, though—in fact, my work was exclusively traditional growing up, as mediums such as oil pastel, graphite, and especially colored pencils were more accessible to me at the time. But as time went on, and the more I was exposed to grunge, 2000s design, and especially mixed media design, the more my work shifted into the digital space. What remains the same, however, is the high expectations I hold upon myself and my work, driving my passion to pursue excellence and ensure quality designs that my clients and myself are proud to showcase. My creative process invests meticulous care and effort into each project, which I hope to highlight in my designs as well.

Outside of design, I enjoy creating and expressing myself through other forms of art—specifically dance, where I’m either choreographing for performances or taking part in some myself. Though not as frequently, I also enjoy capturing the little, easily missable moments that I want to immortalize through photography, and aim to further develop my skills in this field to use in my practice.
